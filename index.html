Color & Origin

Know Your Garnet — The Authentic Look.

Genuine beach-sand Almandine garnet carries a distinctive deep wine-red to burgundy hue, often appearing as reddish-brown to plum in dry bulk and turning vivid crimson when wet or polished. The grains are naturally hard, dense, sub-angular and free from the dull grey of ordinary silica sand.

It originates from black-sand placer deposits formed over millennia along coastal belts, where ocean currents concentrate heavy minerals (ilmenite, rutile, zircon, monazite, sillimanite, garnet) into recognisable dark beach bands.

  • • Mineral family: Almandine (Fe₃Al₂(SiO₄)₃) — the iron-aluminium garnet
  • • Hardness: 7.5–8.0 Mohs — harder than quartz
  • • Specific gravity: ~4.0 — nearly twice that of silica sand
  • • Origin: coastal placer deposits, ocean-sorted over geological time
  • • Inert & non-toxic: safe for water, food-grade and industrial use
