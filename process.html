Behind the Mineral

From Indian Beaches to Global Industry

Every grain of GarnetWorld product moves through a strictly controlled, IRE-aligned value chain. Here is exactly how.

  1. 1 · Beach Sand Mining

    Black-sand placer deposits along recognised coastal belts are dredged using environmentally regulated open-cast methods. Topsoil and overburden are restored after extraction. Reference source belt: east-coast India (Odisha / Andhra Pradesh).

  2. 2 · Wet Concentration at IRE Plant

    At an Indian Rare Earths Limited (IRE) facility, raw beach sand is processed through gravity spirals and shaking tables to concentrate heavy minerals (ilmenite, rutile, zircon, monazite, sillimanite, garnet). Reference plant: IRE OSCOM, Chhatrapur.

  3. 3 · Magnetic & Electrostatic Separation

    High-tension electrostatic and high-intensity magnetic separators isolate Almandine garnet from the heavy mineral concentrate. This is the step where GarnetWorld's purity (≥97%) is born.

  4. 4 · Drying, Sieving & Grading

    Garnet is dried below 0.1% moisture and sieved through calibrated screens into industrial grades — Mesh 30/60, 80, 120 — meeting SAE J444 and customer specs.

  5. 5 · QC, COA & Packaging

    Each batch is sampled in-house & at third-party labs for hardness, chemistry, conductivity and mesh distribution. A Certificate of Analysis ships with every consignment.

  6. 6 · Worldwide Export

    Goods are stuffed and shipped from the nearest deep-sea port under FOB / CIF / CFR Incoterms to buyers worldwide. Lead time is typically 30–45 days from confirmed PO. Documentation includes COA, MSDS, packing list, BL and certificate of origin. Reference port for Indian shipments: Visakhapatnam (Vizag).
